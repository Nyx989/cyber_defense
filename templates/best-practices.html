Attacks & Solutions

1. Brute-Force Attacks

What Happens: Attackers try to guess your password by repeatedly attempting to log in.

Why It's Dangerous: Weak passwords can be cracked easily, giving attackers access to your account.

Solution: Block accounts after 3 failed login attempts for 5 minutes. Use strong passwords with a mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols.

2. SQL Injection

What Happens: Attackers inject malicious SQL queries to manipulate or steal data from databases.

Why It's Dangerous: Sensitive data like user credentials or financial information can be exposed.

Solution: Use parameterized queries and input validation to prevent malicious SQL code execution.

3. Cross-Site Scripting (XSS)

What Happens: Attackers inject malicious scripts into web pages viewed by other users.

Why It's Dangerous: Can steal session cookies, redirect users to malicious sites, or deface websites.

Solution: Sanitize user inputs and implement Content Security Policy (CSP) to block unauthorized scripts.

4. Phishing

What Happens: Attackers trick users into revealing sensitive information like passwords or credit card details.

Why It's Dangerous: Can lead to identity theft, financial loss, or unauthorized account access.

Solution: Educate users to verify links and enable two-factor authentication (2FA) for added security.

5. Unauthorized File Access

What Happens: Attackers gain access to sensitive files or directories on a server.

Why It's Dangerous: Confidential data can be stolen, modified, or deleted.

Solution: Move suspicious files to quarantine and notify the admin. Use strict file permissions and access controls.

6. Packet Sniffing

What Happens: Attackers intercept network traffic to steal sensitive data like passwords or credit card numbers.

Why It's Dangerous: Unencrypted data can be easily captured and misused.

Solution: Monitor TCP packets and block unauthorized traffic. Use encryption (e.g., HTTPS) to secure data in transit.

7. Steganography Exploits

What Happens: Attackers hide malicious data in images or videos to bypass detection.

Why It's Dangerous: Can be used to deliver malware or exfiltrate data.

Solution: Use strong passwords for encoding/decoding hidden messages. Verify the source of media files before decoding.

8. Anomaly Detection

What Happens: Unusual activity indicates a potential security breach.

Why It's Dangerous: Attackers may have already gained access to your system.

Solution: Redirect users to a verification page and send email alerts. Use machine learning to detect anomalies in real-time.
